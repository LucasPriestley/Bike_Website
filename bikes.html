Honda RC213v-s

- Engine size: 999cc
- Power: 159 bhp
- Top speed: 186 mph
- Max Torque: 102 ft-lb
- Bike weight: 170 kg

The Honda RC213V-S really is a MotoGP bike with lights and as close to the real thing as is practical...
A Honda RC213V-S still in its original shipping crate has become the most expensive Japanese bike ever sold at auction
- fetching a whopping total sales price of £182,500.




The beast hitting top speed...listen to that engine scream!




Ducati 1299 Panigale R Final Edition

- Engine size: 1285cc
- Power: 209 bhp
- Top speed: 190 mph
- Max Torque: 104 ft-lb
- Bike weight: 168 kg

With its Superleggera motor and pipes, Panigale R chassis and 1299 S evolution electronics the Final Edition
is the ultimate expression of the Ducati's V-twin superbike.
Blisteringly fast, with handling and brakes to match,
it's a fitting end to the series and comes with everything you'd expect from an 'R' with delicious detailing,
a reassuringly expensive price tag.




Such a dramatic bike...




Aprilia RSV4 RF

- Engine size: 999cc
- Power: 201 bhp
- Top speed: 185 mph
- Max Torque: 85 ft-lb
- Bike weight: 180 kg

Aprilia has joined the 200bhp club with style. Like the previous model the RSV4 RF is incredibly easy to ride fast,
thanks to its even lighter, more accurate handling, tweaked electronics and flexible V4 power.
But with its new engine mods it now has the muscle to compete with the superbike class of 2015.




The newest member of the 200+ bhp, The Aprilia RSV4-RF. An awesome track-focused superbike.




Yamaha YZF-R1 M

- Engine size: 998cc
- Power: 197 bhp
- Top speed: 186 mph
- Max Torque: 82.9 ft-lb
- Bike weight: 201 kg

Not only is it other-worldly rapid, this M version, with its carbon and Öhlins, is knee-tremblingly sexy, too.




This clever engine has the easy, vibe-free flexibility of an electric motor, on and off the throttle,
mated to the kind of ballistic top end you can only really exploit fully on track.




Ronax 500

- Engine size: 499cc
- Power: 160 bhp
- Top speed: 186 mph
- Max Torque: 44.25 ft-lb
- Bike weight: 115 kg

This is the 21-gun salute to the two-stroke 500cc World Championship bikes of that period,
and bears a striking resemblance to the 2001 Honda NSR500 - the last two-stroke engine bike to win a Grand Prix,
in the hands of a certain Valentino Rossi.




Its four-cylinder two-stroke engine cranks out 160bhp at 11,500rpm, with MotoGP-spec
Brembo disc brakes fitted to ensure you ride faster and brake deeper into corners.
